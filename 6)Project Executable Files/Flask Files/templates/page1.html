Wind Turbine Energy Prediction Based on Weather Conditions

Renewable energy refers to energy derived from natural resources that are continuously replenished. Unlike fossil fuels, which are finite and contribute to environmental pollution, renewable energy sources are sustainable and have minimal impact on the planet. These sources include solar energy, wind energy, hydropower, geothermal energy, and biomass. By harnessing these abundant resources, we can reduce our dependence on non-renewable fuels and mitigate climate change.


Wind energy is generated by capturing the kinetic energy of moving air using wind turbines. These turbines consist of large blades mounted on a tower. As the wind blows, it causes the blades to rotate, which drives a generator to produce electricity. Wind farms are strategically located in areas with consistent wind patterns, such as coastal regions or open plains. The efficiency of wind energy production depends on factors like wind speed, turbine design, and maintenance. With advancements in technology, wind power has become a significant contributor to global electricity generation, offering a clean and sustainable alternative to traditional fossil fuels.
